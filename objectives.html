Scientific & methodological goals

Objectives of IONO-DIET.

IONO-DIET is structured around three main scientific objectives that guide the tasks and work packages: understanding LOAI coupling, developing NRT methodologies and moving towards operational applications.

LOAI = Lithosphere–Ocean–Atmosphere–Ionosphere coupling, at the heart of ionospheric seismology and tsunami applications.

Scientific Objectives

From fundamental questions to applications

Objective 1 – LOAI coupling & ionospheric seismology

Improve our understanding of how earthquakes and tsunamis couple into the atmosphere and ionosphere, and how this coupling depends on source parameters (fault geometry, focal mechanism, depth) and background ionospheric/atmospheric conditions. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

This includes documenting the ionospheric, magnetic and airglow response to a wide range of events and characterizing the vertical structure of co-seismic disturbances.

Objective 2 – NRT-compatible methods

Develop near-real-time compatible methods able to detect co-seismic and co-tsunamic ionospheric disturbances, estimate earthquake magnitude and uplift, generate seismo-ionospheric imagery and determine tsunami wave heights from ionospheric data. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

This requires novel detection, spectral analysis and inversion approaches adapted to streaming GNSS data and realistic noise conditions.

Objective 3 – Application & limitations

Apply and test the developed methods in real-time or near-real-time conditions, identify their limitations in the presence of other travelling ionospheric disturbances and space weather, and quantify the accuracy of the retrieved parameters. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

This objective culminates in a prototype demonstration and an online catalogue of natural hazard–driven ionospheric disturbances.

Methodological vision

How the objectives are reached

Multi-instrument observational basis

All tasks rely primarily on GNSS TEC data from dense ground-based receiver networks. They are complemented by magnetometers, LEO satellites, radio occultations and airglow imagers to capture the full vertical structure and spatial extent of the disturbances. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

From retrospective to NRT-compatible approaches

Previous ionospheric studies were mostly retrospective and relied on band-pass filtering. IONO-DIET goes beyond this by developing methods that can work in near-real time and with minimal filtering, enabling timely response for early warning. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

The three objectives are mapped onto three scientific tasks and a set of work packages that progressively move from fundamental understanding to applied, near-real-time methodologies and prototype testing.
