About IONO-DIET

Linking natural hazards and space sciences
◎ Project overview

IONO-DIET is an interdisciplinary project at the interface between natural hazards and space sciences. Its main goal is to build an ionosphere-based system that can detect natural hazards in near-real time and infer key source parameters directly from ionospheric observations.

Priority is given to submarine earthquakes and tsunamis, which are among the most destructive natural hazards worldwide. By exploiting the rapid ionospheric response to acoustic–gravity waves generated by these events, IONO-DIET aims to complement conventional early warning systems.

Techniques include GNSS TEC analysis, ionospheric imaging, parametric studies and new methods for automatic detection and classification of ionospheric disturbances.
Why the ionosphere? The ionosphere reacts to earthquakes and tsunamis within minutes, often well before tsunami waves reach coastlines, opening a time window for additional constraints on tsunami potential and earthquake source properties.

Scientific & Methodological Objectives

Three main axes of the project
1 Fundamental

LOAI coupling & ionospheric seismology

Improve our understanding of lithosphere–ocean–atmosphere–ionosphere (LOAI) coupling and how co-seismic and co-tsunamic disturbances manifest as travelling ionospheric disturbances under different background conditions.

2 Methodological

Near-real-time ionosphere-based methods

Develop NRT-compatible techniques to automatically detect ionospheric signatures of natural hazards, estimate magnitude, uplift, source extent and tsunami wave heights, and distinguish these from other TIDs.

3 Applicational

Towards operational integration

Test the prototype on real-time GNSS data streams in high-risk regions, quantify limitations in disturbed ionospheric conditions, and define practical requirements for an operational system.

In more detail, the project seeks to:

  1. Characterize how CSID and CTID depend on source properties and background ionosphere.
  2. Build an atmosphere–ionosphere transfer concept linking ionospheric signatures to tsunami wave heights.
  3. Design robust NRT tools capable of working during space weather events and in the presence of other TIDs.

Scientific Tasks & Work Packages

From fundamental studies to prototype testing

Task 1 – Fundamental coupling

Investigates the ionospheric, magnetic and airglow response to earthquakes and tsunamis, builds a comprehensive database of events, and establishes quantitative relationships between ionospheric amplitudes and seismic or tsunami parameters.

Includes WP1.1, WP1.2 and WP1.3.

Task 2 – NRT methodologies

Develops near-real-time methods for automatic detection, source parameter estimation, ionospheric imaging and rapid tsunami-height assessment using GNSS TEC data and advanced signal processing.

Includes WP2.1 to WP2.5.

Task 3 – Application & limitations

Builds the first online catalogue of natural-hazard-driven ionospheric disturbances, explores limitations caused by other ionospheric processes, upgrades TEC software and tests the prototype on real-time streams.

Includes WP3.1 to WP3.4.
Management & outreach (Task 4) cover project coordination, reporting to funding agencies, a dedicated web page and international meetings in Brazil and France.

IONO-DIET Concept

From GNSS measurements to tsunami potential

GNSS receivers continuously monitor ionospheric TEC above seismically active regions. When an earthquake occurs, acoustic–gravity waves generated at the surface reach the ionosphere within several minutes, producing co-seismic ionospheric disturbances.

IONO-DIET aims to automatically detect these signals, determine their arrival time, location and amplitude, and invert this information for earthquake magnitude, uplift and tsunami wave heights using pre-established relationships and transfer functions.

The prototype will be tested on real-time data from networks in regions such as Japan, New Zealand, Chile and Sumatra.
